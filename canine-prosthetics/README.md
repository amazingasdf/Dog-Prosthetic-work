# Canine Assistive Prosthetics

**Author:** Vihaan | Orlando Science High School, Class of 2027
**Started:** July 2026 | **Status:** Foundation phase (private repo)

## Goal

Design, prototype, and iterate a 3D-printed below-knee (transtibial) prosthetic socket for dogs, grounded in real mobility needs observed through animal shelter volunteering — and develop it toward a rigorous research question with mentorship from UCF's XRBioSim Lab.

## Approach

1. **Literature base** — read and summarize peer-reviewed work on canine prosthesis biomechanics, osseointegration, and veterinary orthotics
2. **Real-world grounding** — shelter volunteering to observe dogs with mobility issues and collect design requirements from staff
3. **CAD design** — model a transtibial socket in Fusion 360, documenting every design decision
4. **Prototype & iterate** — 3D print, test, photograph failures, revise
5. **Analysis** — FEA stress simulation on the socket geometry
6. **Research direction** — refine into a candidate research question with faculty input
